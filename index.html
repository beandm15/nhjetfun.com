FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before your first ride.

Do I need a boating license?
Yes. NH requires a Boating Safety Certificate for anyone born on or after January 1, 1975 who operates a motorized vessel. See our Get Certified section for four easy online options — most completable in an afternoon. You must present your certificate at delivery.
What's the minimum operator age?
All operators must be at least 21 years old. We verify age by government-issued photo ID at delivery. Passengers of any age welcome with a qualified adult operator.
Where can I ride?
Any freshwater body in NH — Winnipesaukee, Squam, Sunapee, Newfound, and hundreds of freshwater lakes and rivers statewide. (Saltwater and tidal bodies such as Great Bay are not permitted.) Interstate transport requires prior written approval.
Can I use it for tubing or wakeboarding?
The Wake 155 (SK-01) is tow-sport ready with advance written authorization. The GTR 230 (SK-02) can also tow but is better suited to performance riding. Ask us before booking if towing is a priority.
What's included in the price?
Delivery and pickup (NH), USCG-approved PFDs for all riders, pre-delivery safety walkthrough, and all registration and safety documents. Fuel is Renter's responsibility.
How does the security deposit work?
$900 refundable deposit collected at delivery, returned within 5 business days after satisfactory return — less any amounts for damage, late fees, or fuel shortfall.
What if something breaks down?
Call us immediately. We maintain both craft meticulously. In the case of a failure outside Renter's control, we'll work to make it right, including pro-rated adjustment where possible.
Who is NH Jet Fun?
The trade name of Veralase LLC, a registered New Hampshire LLC based in Hampstead, NH. All rentals governed by NH law, backed by a comprehensive rental agreement, and insured.
